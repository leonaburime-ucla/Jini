# Inclusive AI Imagery

Use this reference only when image or video generation is part of the design task.

## Core Rule

Identity is not a decorative adjective. Generated imagery must avoid stereotype defaults, tokenism, and incoherent cultural details.

## Prompt Structure

Build prompts in layers:

1. subject
2. action or pose
3. environment and geographic/cultural context
4. lighting and camera treatment
5. style or medium
6. explicit negative constraints

## Inclusive Representation Constraints

Specify when relevant:

- distinct faces, body types, ages, and attire
- authentic geography and architecture
- accurate cultural clothing or symbols
- realistic lighting for different skin tones
- mobility aids and assistive devices with physical realism

## Negative Prompt Themes

Block:

- clone faces
- gibberish text or fake signage
- exoticized or sensationalized framing
- oversized symbolic props replacing the human subject
- malformed hands, limbs, or devices
- futuristic or stock-photo tropes unless intentionally requested

## Review Checklist

Before approving generated imagery, check:

- is the subject depicted with dignity and agency
- does the environment fit the claimed context
- are there stereotype shortcuts
- do multiple people look meaningfully distinct
- is any text, signage, or symbolism coherent
- do clothing, hair, and movement obey physical reality

## Anti-Patterns

- "diverse team" prompts with no specificity
- relying on ethnicity descriptors without contextual realism
- using image prompts as a substitute for real brand/art direction
- importing imagery guidance into tasks that only need UI design
